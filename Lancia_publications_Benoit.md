## Research Interests

Maria Rosaria Lancia works at the intersection of mathematical analysis, 
numerical analysis, and probability, with a focus on problems exhibiting 
irregularities at different scales. Her main research areas are:

1. **Fractal analysis and fractal geometry**
   - Energy forms and Dirichlet forms on fractal sets (self-similar and non-self-similar)
   - Functional spaces on d-sets and fractals
   - Fractal boundaries of Koch type (Koch curve, Koch snowflake, prefractal domains)
   - Fractafolds and vector analysis on fractal manifolds

2. **PDEs and boundary value problems on irregular domains**
   - Transmission problems across fractal interfaces
   - Venttsel' (Wentzell) and Robin-Venttsel' boundary conditions
   - Parabolic and elliptic problems in domains with fractal boundary
   - Dynamic boundary conditions
   - Semilinear and quasilinear evolution problems

3. **Nonlocal and fractional operators**
   - Regional fractional Laplacian
   - Time-fractional problems (Caputo derivative)
   - Fractional diffusion in irregular domains
   - p-curl systems and p-Laplacian with Venttsel' conditions
   - Anomalous diffusion

4. **Dirichlet forms**
   - Energy forms on fractal curves and surfaces
   - Mosco convergence and Kuwae-Shioya convergence in varying Hilbert spaces
   - Nonlinear energy forms on fractal sets
   - Forms on non-self-similar fractals via Lagrangian integration

5. **Numerical methods and approximation**
   - Finite element methods (FEM) on prefractal domains
   - A priori error estimates for fractal problems
   - Optimal mesh generation for Koch-type boundaries
   - Numerical approximation of transmission problems

6. **Homogenization**
   - Fractal homogenization
   - Insulating and conducting fractal layers
   - Convergence of solutions across fractal mixtures

7. **Subelliptic operators**
   - Hörmander-type subelliptic operators
   - Problems in non-Euclidean variational structures

8. **Applications**
   - Heat flow and diffusion across fractal interfaces
   - Magnetostatic problems in fractal domains
   - Fluid dynamics in fractal channels
   - Optimal shapes for energy harvesting and cooling systems
   - Elasticity (linear and nonlinear)
   - Aerodynamics (early career): boundary integral formulations for potential flows

## Key Recurring Themes Across Her Work

1. **Koch-type boundaries and prefractal approximation:** Most of her PDE problems 
   are set on domains with Koch curve or Koch snowflake boundary; she systematically 
   studies the fractal problem as a limit of prefractal approximating problems.

2. **Mosco convergence and varying Hilbert spaces:** A central technical tool for 
   passing from prefractal to fractal limits, extended also to nonlinear settings.

3. **Venttsel' (Wentzell) boundary conditions:** A recurring class of dynamic 
   boundary conditions that she has studied in elliptic, parabolic, semilinear, 
   nonlocal and time-fractional settings.

4. **Well-posedness + regularity + numerical approximation:** Her papers typically 
   follow this three-part structure: prove existence/uniqueness, establish regularity, 
   then develop and analyze the numerical approximation.

5. **FEM on prefractal domains:** Systematic development of finite element methods 
   adapted to the fractal geometry, with optimal a priori error estimates.
